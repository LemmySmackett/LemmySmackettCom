Once in a secluded mountain temple, Zen master Onanji and his most devoted disciple, a young man named Shikō, meditated for ten days. At the end of the tenth day, the master opened his eyes and declared: "It is time."

"Time?" Shikō opened his own eyes. "Time for what, master?"

Onanji rose. "To determine if you are worthy to continue living as my student." 

"Master?"

"One question will decide your fate."

Shikō gulped and nodded. "I will answer it, master." He steeled himself. "Without hesitation!"

"Very well," the master said. "The question is this: which is superior, to coom or goon?"

Shikō blinked. "To....coom, master?" 

"Or goon."

"But..."

 The master stared down at his disciple. "Are you hesitating?"

"I-I am, but master—" Shikō stammered. "Aren't they the same thing?"

The master sighed. "As I feared." He drew his sword, which he always kept in a saya on his hip. 

"Master?"

"In five years' time you have heard my teachings, slept under my roof, eaten my food—"

"Master, please put that away. Why are you—"

Onanji pointed his blade. "—and you have learned nothing!"

"I beseech you, master, sheathe your sword!" The frightened disciple cried, falling back. "You were stern with me at the range the other day! If my aim has offended you, I will take up the bow and draw until I strike the mato true!"

"Your slipshod attempts at archery are of no concern!" The master snapped. "My question, and the Gates of Hell that lie beyond it, are the only obstacles before you now!"

"Surely this is a jest." Shikō fought back a crooked grin. "Some sort of crude prank—"

"It is no such thing!" The master stepped closer, assuming the middle stance, what is known as Chūdan-no-kamae. "Answer or I will cut you down, here and now!"

"But ma—"

"Speak only the answer or I will claim your tongue before I claim your life!"

The student was at a loss, for his master had always been a kind and even-tempered man. Worse still, he could divine no difference between cooming and gooning. Both were unseemly internet argot for the same vulgar act of self-gratification—an act that Shikō himself was forbidden to partake in, for cardinal indulgences only served to heighten attachment to one's own desires, ku, the source of all suffering.

"Well?" The master said.

Sweat beaded Shikō's brow as he racked his brain, searching desperately for the answer that would assuage the strange distemper that had overtaken Onanji.  He surmised that his master was an ancient sage, a venerable man who had lived through countless seasons—possibly even in his early forties—and so concluded that the antiquated 4chan-coded uncslop slang for waxing the Buddha would prove correct.

"Master!" Shikō sat up in the proper posture, kiza, his back ridged as a sugi trunk. "I have known the answer all along!"

"Then speak it!" 

"To Coom!" Shikō barked from deep within his gut, like a kiai. "It is always superior to coom than to goon!"

A tense silence hung in the air as the men stared at one another. A single bead of sweat ran down the side of Shikō's cheek and clung to the tip of his chin. Outside in the garden, the bamboo shishi-odoshi, now filled with water, slowly tipped and clacked against a rock.

TOK!

The bead leapt.

"That's right," the master finally said.

Shikō exhaled. 

"But it is not enough to speak the correct answer."

"Master?"

"One must also grasp the reason. Otherwise, those words could be a lucky guess—or merely clever intuition."

Shikō paled.

"So...SHIKŌ!" The master raised his blade high, assuming the fire stance, Jōdan-no-kamae. "Tell me why it is better to coom than to goon!"

Shikō pursed his lips but remained seated. He did not flee, he did not flinch, for he knew that with one false move, Onanji would split his skull in half. And so, in body, Shikō remained steadfast, like En no Gyōja standing beneath the freezing waterfalls of the Yoshino–Ōmine Mountains. But his heart gave in to despair. 

_Am I really going to die over cooming. Gooning. Why?_

Shikō had joined the temple to escape cooming and gooning. To escape the relentless pursuit of fruitless pleasures. For had not the whole world devolved into orgiastic onanism—the insatiable, all-consuming goonfest of modern life?

_Why? Why? Why?_

The disciple gazed up at the master, searching his hard face. Like an archer's bowstring, Onanji's whole body remained taut, ready to strike in the flash of a second. A moment of agonizing tension followed by an obliterating release.

_That's it._

"The gooner...is an archer who never releases the bowstring."

The master narrowed his gaze. "What's that?"

"The gooner wants to hold the string taut forever. He aims at his mato—goonfuel cellulite wobblemeat—but does not seek to hit it. Indeed, trapped in his gooncave, he never could. And so, such a man is no archer at all!"

Onanji listened with intent.

"He is a mockery of archers!" The disciple's voice grew louder, building in confidence and intensity. "For an archer nocks each arrow to set it free! To deny release is to deny the arrow its flight, its very purpose! And even if the target is missed, there is a joy to be taken in each attempt. An exhilaration in failure, what the French call goût de l’effort. And one day, goût de l’effort will lead inexorably to l’esprit de conquête!"

"Shikō..."

The disciple sprang to his feet. "And so you see, the gooner represents the ultimate arrested state of man's desire, perpetually seeking to prolong pleasure, denying the release that creates life and death, that sets the Great Cycles in motion! But any man who attempts such cessation is an enemy, not only of his fellow man, but the whole of existence itself!"

"Shikō!"

Veins bulged in the disciple's neck as he shouted at the top of his lungs. "But the coomer is different! The coomer exalts in release, expending his very life force while spilling the seeds that sow life! And one day, someway, somehow, he will spill that seed on fertile ground! So it is that Gooning is the great Nihilism while Cooming is the very Will of God! They couldn't be any more different! Indeed, the demons do not fear the man who goons, they fear the man who has coomed ten million times! Furthermore, as it is written in the Upanishads—"

Onanji stepped forward and embraced his disciple. "That's enough, Shikō."

"M-master?"

"Be at ease, my disciple. I knew from the first day you entered this hallowed temple that you were a true coomer."

"Oh, master!" Shikō fought back tears. "Do you think we should have a synchronized jerk sesh to lewd AI gens of Sabrina Carpenter to celebrate?"

Onanji grimaced. "Don't ruin the moment with that gay gooner shit, Shikō."

"Sorry master."
