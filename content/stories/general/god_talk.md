Oh, my God. I'm talking to God!"

"Hey."

"This is nuts! It's unbelievable!"

"Yeah, yeah. Be not afraid and all that."

"It's just so—"

"Pop an Ativan if you gotta."

"I have so many questions!"

"Humans always do."

"Okay, first off, uuh, why me?"

"Why not?"

"Am I a prophet? This is like a vision, right, so there must be some divine message of galactic, celestial, incomprehensibly immense importance you want me to deliver to the world as your personally anointed messenger. Right?"

"Eh? Oh, no, no."

"No?"

"Nothing like that."

"Oh."

"I haven't done the prophet thing for centuries. A millennium, even."

"...oh."

"Not that that stops anybody."

"Um."

"And it's not like you people listen anyway."

"Okay, that's a little condescending."

"Really? 'Cause it's hard for me not to condescend considering I'm, ya know—"

"God."

"Mhm."

"No, I get it, it's just—"

"I'm literally descending into a lower plane of reality to have this conversation with you right now."

"And I appreciate that, my LORD."

"It's hard not to condescend when you descend for a conversation."

"And I appreciate that too."

"I could talk to you on my level, you know? Do you have any idea what would happen to your face if you heard my true voice?"

"Er, Ark of the Covenant in Indiana Jones?"

"Ark of the Covenant in Indiana Jones."

"I've never actually seen the film, but I caught an AI recap on YouTube."

"AI recap?"

"Uh huh. They redid the whole movie in five minutes with fruits and cartoon animals."

"Really."

"Major Arnold Ernest Toht was a dancing purple penguin wearing a totenkopf schirmmütze."

"Wonders never cease."

"They opened the box, and his cute little fascist beak melted clean off."

"Can't believe you haven't seen the original..."

"Gruesome stuff."

"Right, well, ILM VFX artist Chris Walas was in charge of the *original* face-melting effect, and I sent him nightmares for weeks just to make sure he got it right."

"That's, uh, very conscientious of you?"

"It was."

"So what I'm hearing right now *isn't* your true voice."

"Right. This is my false voice."

"That explains the falsetto."

"Mhm."

"I always expected the One Above All to have more vocal gravitas and sound less, uh, Bee Gees."

"You were expecting the bass."

"Right."

"You couldn't handle the bass, Ace. I'd bass your face clean off."

"At least I'm staying alive. So, uh, why?"

"Why everything? Well—"

"No, why are we having this conversation?"

"Hm?"

"If you don't have some divine revelation or a new gospel you want me to deliver to the world—"

"No, no. Pretty happy with the current one."

"—then why are we talking?"

"And honestly, if I did have a new gospel, I'd find someone with an established platform to distribute it."

"Established platform?"

"Like Joe Rogan. Or IShowSpeed."

"You'd pick IShowSpeed as your divine prophet?"

"Sure. Did you see the traps on his Black Flash cosplay?"

"..."

"Shit was crazy."

"..."

"Anyway, who says the First Mover has to have a reason for moving?"

"I guess that makes sense..."

"You humans are always so wrapped up in cause and effect, meaning, purpose."

"It would be nice to know."

"Truth told, I just love a good chat."

"You do?"

"Sure. Who doesn't?"

"There's like a thousand books and a bunch of movies grappling with the profound agony of your insufferable silence."

"Yeah, well—"

"Scorsese's Silence. A Serious Man by the Coen Brothers. The Seventh Seal."

"Yeah, well, you saw the kind of movies that guy made. Would you want to talk to Ingmar Bergman?"

"I suppose not. Coen Brothers seem cool though." 

"I like Joel."

"So you just go around talking to people? For kicks?"

"Mhm. Like Bill Murray."

"Huh?"

"No one will ever believe you."

"I guess that's true."

"It's alright. Nobody ever believes in me either. That's kind of the problem."

"Well, you could just tell everyone you exist."

"And ruin the experiment?"

"Experiment?"

"Look, I'm God, right?"

"As far as I know."

"I'm omniscient, omnipresent, and omnipotent. I know everything everywhere, and I always have and I always will."

"That's what they told me in Sunday school."

"But what if I don't?"

"Uh."

"What if there's something outside of everything? And I don't know about it?"

"With all due respect, LORD, that sounds a bit sacrilegious."

"It is, and I would send myself to hell for even thinking about it."

"Wow."

"But I can't because I'm God, so here I am thinking about it."

"This is heavy. I had no idea you grappled with this kind of stuff. Like us."

"Eh, apples and trees."

"So you think there might be, like, a Greater God?"

"I don't know. And that's why I created humans."

"Hm?"

"I wanted to see if you guys could figure out I exist without me completely revealing myself. Like rabbits raised in a terrarium with blacked-out windows."

"But you're talking—"

"Bill Murray."

"Oh. Right."

"I wanted to see if humanity, as a whole, could come to the correct conclusion that I exist purely by observing reality itself, which is obviously designed and constructed by a superior intelligence."

"Is it?"

"There's an enzyme embedded in the inner membrane of your mitochondria that literally looks like a spinning motor. What do you think, dingus?"

"Condescending again..."

"But it turns out you don't and you won't."

"Hm?"

"As education and intelligence rises, people turn into atheists. Or they become non-practicing agnostic whatevers. Like you."

"Sorry...?"

"You know what the problem with intelligence is?"

"Um."

"It's great for coming up with plausible explanations—a million Occam's razors for anything and everything."

"It is a handy heuristic."

"You know what's not a plausible explanation?"

"Er, that you exist?"

"That I exist."

"I suppose that's true. I'm also now remembering that I was in a car accident."

"See, here we go."

"Is this a coma?"

"Yes, you're in a coma. I have all my best conversations in comas, okay?"

"That would explain the endless white void."

"Some times I even give tours of paradise."

"So...you're probably not God."

"Well..."

"You're probably just a figment of my imagination."

"Who's to say?"

"I'm just talking to myself."

"Depending on who you ask, God is just the Collective Manifestation of the Universal Will, so talking to anyone, let alone God, is always—"

"—talking to myself."

"There you go. You're gonna wake up soon, by the way."

"Oh. That's good."

"They had to amputate."

"Oh. That's bad." 

"Mhm."

"Was it my—"

"Not your willy."

"Thank God."

"You're welcome."

"Uh, well, thanks for the chat. I hope you figure out that whole Greater God thing."

"Yeah, not banking on it. I think the harsh truth is that he'd literally have to reveal himself like—"

"Hey."

"Oh, my God!"

"Oh, my God!"

"Fellas, please."

"It's God!"

"The Greater God!"

"Double G is fine."

"I can't believe this is happening! I have so many questions!"

"Easy, buddy. Us deities don't have Ativan."

"Tell me, please! What's out there?! Why reveal yourself now?!"

"And why do you look like Bill Murray?"

"Because I decided the time was right. And because no one will ever believe you."
